Carbonate vs. Silicate Weathering

Carbonate vs. Silicate Weathering

  • Carbonate Weathering:
    • Dissolves carbonate minerals on land
    • Increases ocean carbonate
      • Adds twice as much carbonate to oceans as silicate weathering
      • Relieves ocean acidification
      • Increases transfer of CO2 from atmosphere to ocean
      • Creates carbonate rocks on sea floor
        with carbon that originated on land
    • Does not transform atmospheric CO2 to rocks
  • Silicate Weathering:
    • Transforms carbon dioxide in atmosphere into rocks
      • Creates carbonate rocks on sea floor
        with carbon that originated in atmosphere

Equilibrium Balances

At Equilibrium:

  • Silicate weathering = Rate of CO2 emission

    • (Before fossil fuels, CO2 emission was just volcanic degassing)

  • Total weathering (carbonate + silicate) = Rate of carbonate burial on sea-floor

Prospects for future:

  • Oceanic sinks:
    • A few decades:
      • Surface ocean saturates
      • Further absorption limited by conveyor belt overturning
    • A few centuries:
      • Around 50% of excess CO2 dissolves into deep oceans
      • Dissolution stops as deep ocean acidifies
    • A few thousand years:
      • Reactions with limestone restore pH, CO2 solubility
    • Tens to hundreds of thousands of years
      • Carbonate-rock weathering adds CO3-2 to oceans.
      • Silicate-rock weathering removes & buries all excess CO2.
  • Bottom line:
    • After 1000 years, around 30% of excess CO2 remains in atmosphere
    • After 10,000 years, 13% remains
    • After 100,000 years, 6% remains

Temperature Anomaly

  • Global temperature change:
    • Average temperatures are different at different places.
    • Temperatures change with the seasons
    • How to compare temperature change between places with different climates?
  • Temperature anomaly:
    • Define a reference time period (several decades)
    • Anomaly = actual temperature at a place and time
      minus average temperature at that place during reference period

Temperature Anomaly

  • Anomaly = actual temperature at a place and time
    minus average temperature at that place during reference period
  • Example: Anomaly for Nashville, August, 2018
    • Monthly avg. temp. for August, 2018 = \(26.7^\circ\mathrm{C}\)
    • Average August temp 1981–2010 = \(25.9^\circ\mathrm{C}\)
    • Anomaly = \(26.7^\circ\mathrm{C} - 25.9^\circ\mathrm{C} = 0.8^\circ\mathrm{C}\)
